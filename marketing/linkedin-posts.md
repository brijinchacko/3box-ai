## Week 1, Post 2: Industry Insight

**75% of resumes are rejected by ATS before a human sees them.**

Here's what most people don't know about Applicant Tracking Systems:

1. ATS doesn't read your resume like a human. It parses keywords, sections, and formatting separately.

2. A "creative" resume with columns, tables, or images often scores WORSE than a simple one-column layout.

3. Keyword matching isn't just about having the right words — it's about having them in the right context and frequency.

4. Many ATS systems now use AI to understand context (not just exact keyword matches), but basic keyword optimization still matters.

5. Your resume might look perfect in Word/PDF but completely break when parsed by common ATS platforms.

What you can do:
- Use a standard, clean layout (no columns, no tables)
- Mirror the exact language from the job description
- Include both the full term AND the acronym (e.g., "Artificial Intelligence (AI)")
- Test your resume against job descriptions before submitting

## Week 3, Post 5: Educational Content

**The STAR method is good. The CARL method might be better.**

Everyone knows STAR for behavioral interviews:
- Situation → Task → Action → Result

But I've been coaching candidates with the CARL method:
- Context → Action → Result → Learning

Why CARL works better:

1. "Context" replaces "Situation + Task" — it's more concise and includes both
2. "Learning" at the end shows self-awareness and growth mindset
3. Interviewers remember the ending — and "what I learned" is more memorable than just "the result"

Example:

STAR: "Our team was behind on a project deadline (S). I needed to improve our velocity (T). I implemented daily standups and a prioritization framework (A). We delivered on time with 98% quality score (R)."

CARL: "Our team was behind on a project deadline (C). I implemented daily standups and a ruthless prioritization framework (A). We delivered on time with 98% quality score (R). I learned that early process intervention is always cheaper than late-stage heroics — now I build check-in rhythms into every project from day one (L)."

Same story. But CARL makes you sound like someone who grows from every experience.

## Video 2: ATS Resume Tips
**Duration:** 5-6 minutes
**Title:** How to Beat ATS in 2026: 7 Resume Tips That Actually Work (+ Free Checker)

### Script

**[0:00-0:20] Hook**
"75% of resumes get rejected before a human ever sees them. They're filtered out by software called ATS — Applicant Tracking Systems. Today I'll show you exactly how to beat them, with 7 tips that actually work in 2026."

**[0:20-1:00] What is ATS?**
"ATS stands for Applicant Tracking System. It's software that companies use to manage job applications. Think of it as a filter — before your resume reaches a recruiter, it goes through this software that parses, scores, and ranks it against other applicants. Companies like Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, and iCIMS all make ATS software, and they all work slightly differently."

**[1:00-5:00] 7 Tips (1 minute each)**

"Tip 1: Use a clean, single-column layout. Tables, columns, and text boxes break ATS parsing. Stick to a simple top-to-bottom layout."

"Tip 2: Use standard section headings. 'Professional Experience' not 'My Journey.' 'Education' not 'Academic Adventures.' ATS looks for specific heading patterns."

"Tip 3: Mirror the job description's exact language. If the job says 'project management,' your resume should say 'project management,' not 'managing projects' or 'PM.'"

"Tip 4: Include both acronyms AND full terms. Write 'Search Engine Optimization (SEO)' not just 'SEO.' Some ATS match on full terms, others on acronyms."

"Tip 5: Save as PDF unless specified otherwise. Modern ATS handles PDF well. If the job posting specifically says Word format, use that."

"Tip 6: Quantify everything. 'Increased sales by 34%' is both ATS-friendly (keyword: sales) and human-friendly (impressive metric)."

"Tip 7: Test before you submit. Use a free ATS checker to score your resume against the specific job description. I built one at 3box.ai/tools/ats-checker — it's completely free, no signup needed."
